# Unit 8: Principles of Cyber Security

## Unit Topic

This unit covers core cybersecurity principles: threat modeling,
encryption, risk assessment, and governance frameworks (GDPR, ISO/IEC
27001), alongside common vulnerabilities like malware, phishing, and data
breaches, and the professional and ethical considerations around user
privacy and regulatory compliance.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion of this unit, I should be able to:

- Identify and analyze cybersecurity challenges in modern enterprise
  systems.
- Evaluate tools, techniques, and methodologies for mitigating
  cybersecurity threats.
- Articulate legal, ethical, and professional issues in cybersecurity.

## Formative Assignment

*(Not submitted)*

Analyze a hypothetical enterprise cybersecurity scenario, conduct a
threat assessment, and propose a mitigation plan (security protocols,
encryption, GDPR/ISO 27001 compliance) in a 500-word report.

## Reflection

Unlike Unit 7, there is no mismatch to point to here, this unit's
formative task genuinely matches its own stated outcomes. I still chose
not to submit it, for a different, more direct reason: I have been
conducting threat and risk assessments professionally for a decade. If I
felt I needed to prove that to a tutor, or if this were summative work
rather than formative, I would have done it regardless. But I have to
make constant choices about competing priorities, and completing a
formative exercise I already have extensive real-world background in,
purely for the sake of completion, was not a good use of limited time
against everything else this module and my professional life were
asking of me at once.

This is a genuinely different kind of decision than Unit 7's, that one
was skipped partly because the task was misaligned with its own unit;
this one is skipped on its own merits, a straightforward judgment that
the exercise would not have taught me anything the professional work
already has.
