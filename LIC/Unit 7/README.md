# Unit 7: Principles of Artificial Intelligence (AI) II

## Unit Topic

This unit continues Unit 6's focus on AI principles: automation,
predictive analytics, and natural language processing, with continued
attention to the ethical challenges of AI adoption, bias, transparency,
and accountability.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion of this unit, I should be able to:

- Identify and critically analyze the challenges and opportunities of
  integrating AI into business systems.
- Evaluate AI methodologies and techniques to solve computing challenges
  in enterprise contexts.
- Articulate the legal, social, and ethical implications of AI
  technologies.

## Formative Assignment

*(Not submitted)*

Identify potential security threats and vulnerabilities (phishing,
malware, data breaches), suggest three mitigating security measures, and
submit a 500-word security risk assessment report.

## Reading List

Nielson, S.J. (2023) *Discovering Cybersecurity: A Technical Introduction
for the Absolute Beginner*. Berkeley, CA: Apress. Chapters 1-6.

## Reflection

I completed the readings and lecturecast for this unit but made a
deliberate choice not to submit the formative assignment itself. Two
things led to that decision.

First, this unit's formative task, a security risk assessment covering
phishing, malware, and mitigations like encryption and multi-factor
authentication, is genuinely misaligned with both the unit's own stated
learning outcomes and its assigned reading. The learning outcomes here
are entirely AI-focused, identical to Unit 6's, and Nielson's *Discovering
Cybersecurity* reading covers general cybersecurity fundamentals, neither
maps onto the AI-specific work the outcomes actually describe. Completing
the task as written would not have evidenced this unit's own stated
outcomes even if I had done it.

Second, and separately, the task's actual subject matter, basic security
risk assessment, is territory I already operate in daily, professionally.
Even set against the correct learning outcomes, this specific exercise
was not something I needed to be challenged on the way most of this
module has consistently challenged me. Between a task misaligned with
its own unit and a task unlikely to represent genuine progression even if
it had been aligned, this was the right place in the module to
deliberately deprioritize, rather than complete something for its own
sake.
