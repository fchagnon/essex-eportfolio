# Unit 11: Future-Proofing IT Systems: Integrating Risk Management and Strategic IT Governance

## Unit Topic

This final unit brings together the module's strategic and operational
threads through the lens of ethical governance, compliance, and
organizational resilience, considering TOGAF, COBIT, and ITIL alongside
socio-technical risk, regulatory obligations (GDPR, ISO 27005, NIS2), and
the ethical and societal responsibilities of computing professionals.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion of this unit, I should be able to:

- Assess resilience strategies and systemic risks within socio-technical
  computing environments.
- Evaluate frameworks and best practices to promote ethical, compliant,
  and secure IT operations.
- Articulate the ethical, legal, and professional responsibilities of
  computing professionals in enterprise risk and resilience planning.

## Reflection

Of the four units I skipped in this stretch, this is the one I regret
most, and the one where the reason is different again. Reading this
brief now feels like reading it for the first time; I genuinely don't
remember engaging with it during the module itself. At this point I had
made the decision to redirect all remaining effort toward the final
summative assignment, and this unit's formative work simply didn't
survive that reprioritization.

Unlike Units 8 and 9, this wasn't a defensible call about where my
existing expertise made an exercise redundant, this was territory
(socio-technical risk, ethical governance, the societal responsibilities
of computing professionals under frameworks like NIS2) that would have
been genuinely, intellectually stimulating to engage with. That makes
this a real loss, not a reasonable trade-off. If there's a value in
rebuilding these READMEs this carefully, months after the fact, it's
moments exactly like this one: seeing a gap I didn't fully register at
the time, and using that recognition to be a better, more consistently
engaged student going forward.
