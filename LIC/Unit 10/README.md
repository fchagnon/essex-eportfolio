# Unit 10: Strategic and Enterprise IT Management: Aligning IT with Business Goals

## Unit Topic

This unit shifts from Unit 9's strategic foundations to how IT
governance frameworks (TOGAF, COBIT, ITIL) are operationalised
day-to-day, particularly around risk management, disaster recovery, and
business continuity during periods of change or disruption, alongside
the legal, ethical, and professional considerations around system
continuity.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion of this unit, I should be able to:

- Analyze strategic and operational risks in enterprise IT environments.
- Evaluate frameworks and operational tools for ensuring resilience,
  recovery, and IT-business alignment.
- Discuss the legal and ethical responsibilities involved in continuity
  planning and systems assurance.

## Reflection

Unlike the previous three units, I don't have a genuinely defensible
reason for skipping this one, and it's worth being honest about that
distinction rather than reaching for one after the fact. This wasn't a
deliberate call about where formative effort was best spent; it was
closer to disengagement. I have real, substantial experience in IT
strategy, governance, and risk, both professionally and from
undergraduate study, and at this point in the module I can honestly
recognize that I was likely checking out, treating the material as
compressed review of things I already knew rather than staying engaged
with the specific discussion in front of me. That's a different failure
mode than Units 8 and 9, familiarity with the subject matter became an
excuse to disengage rather than a reason to make a considered choice
about where to focus, and I should have contributed regardless.
