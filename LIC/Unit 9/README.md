# Unit 9: Computing and Risk

## Unit Topic

This unit introduces the strategic dimension of enterprise IT
management, how governance frameworks like TOGAF, COBIT, and ITIL are
used at the executive and planning level to align IT strategy with
organizational objectives, digital transformation, IT portfolio
management, and enterprise architecture.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion of this unit, I should be able to:

- Critically analyze strategic IT governance challenges within complex
  enterprise systems.
- Synthesize insights from governance frameworks to propose
  enterprise-level IT strategies.
- Evaluate enterprise architecture models for aligning IT capabilities
  with long-term business objectives.

## Reflection

This is the third formative assignment in a row I chose not to submit,
and it's worth being honest that this is now a pattern, not an isolated
decision, while also being clear that each has a genuinely distinct
reason behind it rather than one excuse repeated three times.

This one has the strongest professional grounding of the three: twelve
years working with Info-Tech Research Group included running several
workshops directly helping CIOs build disaster recovery plans and
business continuity plans. There's a real part of me that would have
enjoyed showcasing that work. But I'm not enrolled in this program to
demonstrate skills I already have, I'm here to learn new things and
progress my abilities in areas where I actually have room to grow.
Spending formative effort on a DRP exercise would have been performance,
not development.

Were this summative rather than formative, I am confident I would have
completed it, and completed it well, the decision here is specifically
about where formative effort is genuinely well spent, not a general
unwillingness to do the work.
